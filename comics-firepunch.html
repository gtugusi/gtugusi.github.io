Fire Punch (2016)

Many comics, like many other works of fiction, follow conventional storytelling ideas that some of us may predict. Tatsuki Fujimoto's Fire Punch is a testament to the pure rejection of those conventions.

Describing the story of this relatively short series is a losing battle, primarily because Fire Punch changes its premise and plot so much that it makes it completely indescribable. I guarantee, that even just reading the first out of eight volumes will not give you an accurate enough image of what's to come.

One of the author's strongest suits is telling a visual story without employing text unless absolutely necessary. There are quite a few chapters in Fire Punch that feature no text whatsoever, and yet those are some of this manga's strongest and most powerful chapters.

If you are not one who gets queasy easily and if you like trippy but meaningful stories, then Fire Punch is the ideal series for you. Depending on how fast you read it, you can finish the whole series in 4-6 hours.
