Shadow of The Vampire (2000)

Vampires have been part of worldwide folklore for many years, spawning a variety of stories about them. One of those was a 1922 silent film called Nosferatu, a loose re-telling of Bram Stoker's Dracula. The movie is still remembered today as a classic, cementing many myths and cliches.

One thing that made the silent film particularly memorable was the appearance of the actor playing the vampire. Shadow of The Vampire seeks to show an alternative look at the filming of Nosferatu, where the director hired a real vampire to play the part.

This movie's premise immediately grabbed me, since it took a topic many film-nuts are familiar with and twisted it in an interesting and unique way. William Dafoe's performance as Max Schreck results in one of the most believable vampires in cinema.

Shadow of The Vampire gets a strong recommendation from me for all vampire movie lovers out there!
