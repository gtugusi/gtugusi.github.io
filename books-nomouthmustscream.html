I Have No Mouth & I Must Scream (1967)

The sci-fi genre is often used to tell action and adventure stories like Star Wars and Star Trek. However, I think sci-fi can be quite effective at existential questions and themes.

One of my personal favorite sci-fi novels is I Have No Mouth & I Must Scream, a short story set in a post-apocaliptic setting where an AI called AM wiped out nearly all humanity, leaving only a select few who are now tortured for eternity.

What makes this book so effective is how it takes its nihilistic approach to the question of humanity's self-determination. Out of all characters, AM is perhaps the most interesting and developed, providing an uncomfortable reflection upon humankind.

If you like sci-fi horror, then I Have No Mouth & I Must Scream is a must-read for you.
